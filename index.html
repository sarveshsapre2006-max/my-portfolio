01About

I'm an Electronics Engineering student who likes taking a concept from theory to a working circuit — whether that means simulating a physiological signal in MATLAB, wiring up a sensor on an Arduino, or writing the logic that ties it together in Python and C.

My biomedical engineering coursework led me to build a pulse oximeter simulation, modeling how light absorption in blood translates into the PPG waveform you see animating above — the same principle behind the sensor on a hospital fingertip clip.

Outside coursework, I keep building small hardware projects to stay hands-on with the fundamentals: sensors, microcontrollers, and the code that drives them.
